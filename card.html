Snow Leopard

Unlike other big cats, the snow leopard is a non-aggressive animal that when threatened by another predator, may choose to back away. Reports of snow leopards attacking or killing humans are unheard of; in fact, snow leopards are known to avoid human presence more than any other big cat.

  • Scientific name: Panthera uncia
  • Mass: 32 kg (Adult)
  • Length: 1.1 m (Adult)
  • Habitat: Mountain ranges of Central and South Asia

This endagered species is found in altitudes ranging from 3500 meters, to 5000 meters above sea level in summer. They show several adaptations to living in such harsh enviroment: stocky body, thick fur, small and rounded ears - features that help minimize heat loss.
